The Hammam Ritual

Four acts.
One transformation.

The Moroccan hammam ritual is not rushed. It asks patience of you — and rewards it tenfold.

01
Cleanse
Apply warm Savon Beldi Noir to damp skin. Allow the black soap to soften the outermost layer. Rest in warmth.
02
Reveal
Work the Kessa glove across the body in long, deliberate strokes. Watch the skin release what no longer serves it.
03
Restore
Apply Rhassoul clay, mixed to a paste with rose water. Leave for fifteen minutes. Rinse. Breathe.
04
Nourish
While the skin is still warm, press pure Argan oil into every surface. Seal with Amber attar on pulse points.

The Earth's Archive

— 001

Moroccan Argan
Argania spinosa

The argan tree lives for up to 200 years in southwestern Morocco's semi-arid Sous Valley. Its oil — pressed by Berber women using methods unchanged for generations — is the most prized cosmetic oil on earth. Rich in oleic acid, vitamin E and squalene. We source exclusively from certified women's cooperatives.

— 002

Atlas Rhassoul
Lava Clay

Mined from deep volcanic seams beneath the Atlas mountains, Rhassoul clay has been used in hammams since the 8th century. Its negatively charged ions attract and bind to impurities. Uniquely, it conditions as it cleanses — leaving no stripped sensation, only softness.
